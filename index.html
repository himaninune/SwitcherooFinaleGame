how to play

This is a card game played by two players with age group of 10 years or above. The deck consists of X (180 to begin with) Switcheroo cards (95 Subject cards and 78 Letter Cards) On the screen, the players see a random subject card (faced up), a random Letter card (faced up) and the deck of cards (faced down). Each player will deal with 10 cards. Step 1: Player-1 starts the game. Step 2: Look at your cards (dealt to you) and do one of the two options: Option 1: Use a Letter card and come up with a word that answers the card that is face up in the Subject pile. Drag and drop your card on top of the Letter pile. Example: If the Subject card says "Something found in a bathroom", then Drag and drop Letter card "T" and say "Toilet" or drag and drop Letter card "S' and say "Soap". Option 2: Use a Subject card in your hand and come up with a word that starts with the Letter that is face up in the Letter pile. Drag and drop your Subject card on top of the Subject pile. Example: If the Letter card says "B", then drag and drop the Subject card "Something that has seeds" and say "Blackberry". Step 3: If the opponent/Player-2 does not object to your answer, the play continues to the next player. If the opponent/Player-2 challenges the answer or an unacceptable answer will result in the player drawing 1 card from the main deck as a penalty. The first player to get rid of his/her cards wins the game!!! Note A: The players can use wild cards that are either dealt or drawn while playing. With the wild card, the player can either put down 2 cards on his/her turn, play reverse direction, gain more time or switch cards between the players. Note B: Once a word is used as an answer ( i.e Cricket), it cannot be repeated again in the game. The player must draw 1 card as a penalty. This game improves General Knowledge, English vocabulary and quick-wittedness of the players (especially kids and teens!).
